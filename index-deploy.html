Ganesh Shiwakoti

Ph.D. Candidate in Mathematics & Statistics · Florida Atlantic University

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Mathematics & Statistics at Florida Atlantic University, working at the intersection of medical AI, compressed sensing, and biostatistics. My research interests include accelerated medical imaging, deep learning for clinical decision support, survival analysis, and reliable AI for healthcare.

I am affiliated with the Machine Perception and Cognitive Robotics Lab and work as a graduate research assistant at the Rubin and Cindy Gruber AI Sandbox.

Before joining FAU, I taught mathematics for nearly a decade across Nepal, from secondary schools to engineering colleges. I think deeply about the mathematical structures behind learning algorithms, and I enjoy building methods that can be tested on real medical data.

Research focus

What I work on

01 / Imaging

Medical AI

Deep learning methods for medical image classification, representation learning, reconstruction, and clinical decision support across MRI, ultrasound, and X-ray modalities.

02 / Reconstruction

Compressed sensing

Sparse recovery, low-rank modelling, and undersampled MRI reconstruction, with a focus on connecting mathematical theory to practical imaging pipelines.

03 / Inference

Compressed inference

Quantization, pruning, and model compression for deploying deep learning models on constrained clinical and research hardware.

04 / Stats

Biostatistics

Survival analysis, time-to-event modelling, calibration, and uncertainty-aware clinical prediction for biomedical cohorts.

05 / Theory

Deep learning theory

Mathematical questions behind generalization, optimization, loss landscapes, and implicit regularization in over-parameterized models.

06 / Dynamics

Dynamical systems

Iteration of nonlinear maps, chaos, and dynamical perspectives on learning algorithms.
